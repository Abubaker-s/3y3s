What is Fundamental of Machine learning

Machine learning (ML) is built on several core fundamentals that enable systems to learn from data and make predictions or decisions. The process begins with
**data**, which serves as the foundationâ€”raw information is processed into meaningful **features** and **labels** for training models.
**Algorithms**, such as linear regression, decision trees, or neural networks,
form the backbone of ML by identifying patterns in the data. The **training phase**
involves adjusting model parameters to minimize errors, often using optimization techniques like
**gradient descent** and **loss functions** to measure performance. After training, models are **evaluated**
using metrics like accuracy, precision, and recall to ensure they generalize well to unseen data.
A critical challenge is balancing the **bias-variance tradeoff** to prevent
**overfitting** (memorizing training data) or **underfitting** (failing to learn patterns). Finally,
**optimization** techniques, including hyperparameter tuning and regularization, refine models for better performance.
Together, these principles enable ML systems to adapt and improve over time.
