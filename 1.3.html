Common issues in machine learning

Overfitting – The model performs well on training data but poorly on unseen data due to memorizing noise or overly complex patterns.
Solutions include regularization (L1/L2), dropout (for neural networks), and cross-validation.
Underfitting – The model is too simple to capture underlying patterns, leading to poor performance on both training and test data.
Fixes include using more complex models, adding features, or reducing regularization.
Data Quality Issues – Problems like missing values, noise, outliers, and imbalanced datasets can degrade model performance. Techniques like imputation, outlier removal,
and resampling (SMOTE for imbalance) help mitigate these issues.
Bias-Variance Tradeoff – High bias (oversimplification) leads to underfitting, while high variance (overcomplexity) leads to overfitting.
Balancing this tradeoff is crucial for generalization.
Feature Selection & Engineering – Irrelevant or redundant features can reduce model efficiency. Dimensionality reduction (PCA, t-SNE) and feature importance analysis help improve performance
